The Aztec Legend of the Agave Plant and Tequila

By Amy Dean | Cactus

According to The Aztecs when the earth began the evil goddess of the sky, Tzintzimitl, kept the earth in darkness and forced people to make human sacrifices in exchange for light.

Quetzalcoatl, the 'Feathered Serpent," ascended to the sky to fight her but since he couldn’t find her but found Mayahuel is the goddess of fertility and after falling in vole with her took her back to earth. On the run from the evil goddess the lovers found that they had nowhere else to hide so they became trees. One beside the other one, they would forever caress when the wind blew. They were found though and Mayahuel was killed, Quetzalcoatl buried her and flew to the sky and killed the evil goddess. Light returned to earth but Quetzalcoatl would cry every night at his lover’s grave. The other gods took pity on him and made a very special flower grow on Mayahuel’s grave. It had hallucinogenic properties so when Quetzalcoatl drank from it he would find comfort. This is how the agave plant came to be, it’s purpose to comfort the soul of those who have lost someone they love.
